# Bold Design System

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere

Bold should feel like a premium operating system for serious education businesses: editorial restraint up front, technical credibility underneath, and just enough warmth to avoid sterile SaaS minimalism. The visual mood is "quiet confidence with operational sharpness."

The page should read like a poster first and a landing page second. The first viewport is not a dashboard. It is a strong headline, a narrow column of convincing copy, and one dominant product artifact that proves the product is real.

Core atmosphere:

- Warm mineral background instead of stark white
- Near-black ink instead of true black
- Mint as the primary signal color
- Selective cobalt and brass accents for contrast, not brand sprawl
- Rounded geometry, but never bubbly
- Surfaces feel laminated and tactile, not glassy-for-the-sake-of-it

## 2. Color Palette & Roles

### Primary

- `#fdfaf3` Warm canvas background
- `#ffffff` Primary elevated surface
- `#151515` Main heading and button text
- `#5f564d` Body copy
- `#93887c` Muted technical labels

### Signal Colors

- `#43c6a6` Primary accent, CTAs, success energy
- `#187963` Accent ink on soft mint surfaces
- `rgba(67,198,166,0.14)` Accent wash and underlines
- `#145cff` Secondary technical accent for context-aware intelligence
- `rgba(20,92,255,0.10)` Soft cobalt surface tint
- `#d1b27a` Warm brass for case-study emphasis
- `#2c1f14` Deep brass-brown background for featured story sections

### Neutrals & Lines

- `rgba(19,15,11,0.08)` Standard borders
- `rgba(19,15,11,0.14)` Stronger borders and nav chrome
- `#f4eee3` Secondary warm surface
- `#0d1511` Dark forest footer / dark CTA background
- `rgba(255,255,255,0.66)` Muted text on dark surfaces

### Functional Roles

- Mint is for action, signal, and positive system state
- Cobalt is for technical intelligence and context-aware features
- Brass is only for special proof moments like case studies
- Dark forest is reserved for ROI, footer, and high-intent CTA zones

## 3. Typography Rules

### Font Families

- Primary sans: Plus Jakarta Sans
- Monospace accent: DM Mono

### Hierarchy

- Hero display: very large, tight, heavy, `tracking: -0.06em` to `-0.08em`
- Section headlines: large, compressed, `tracking: -0.04em` to `-0.05em`
- Body copy: comfortable and airy, `line-height: 1.8`
- Eyebrows and system labels: mono, uppercase, generous tracking

### Typographic Principles

- Headlines should feel engineered, not playful
- Keep text columns narrow; long copy should never span wide
- Use mono sparingly for metadata, timing, categories, and signals
- Avoid decorative italic or novelty display treatments

## 4. Component Stylings

### Navigation

- Fixed announcement bar above a blurred translucent nav
- Nav should feel precise and light, not chunky
- Primary CTA in nav uses dark fill on light surfaces

### Buttons

- Primary button: mint fill, dark text, subtle lift on hover
- Secondary button: translucent white or quiet outline, never heavy chrome
- Buttons are pill-shaped, but not inflated

### Cards / Panels

- Default stance is cardless layout
- Use panels only when they hold product proof, structured content, or interaction
- Panels have soft shadows and fine borders, not loud elevation
- Rounded corners should be generous: roughly `24px` to `40px`

### Product Demo

- The product artifact should feel like a real interface, not a fake browser frame
- Use precise spacing, mono metadata, timestamp chips, and believable message rhythm
- Proof beats decoration

### Data Surfaces

- Use thin dividers, grid backgrounds, and soft radial glows
- Keep metrics simple and legible; avoid overdesigned chart junk

## 5. Layout Principles

- Overall max width: around `1200px`
- Horizontal rhythm should feel roomy on desktop and tightly controlled on mobile
- Section spacing is generous; the design should breathe
- Major sections are separated by borders or strong surface changes
- Alternate between open composition and denser proof blocks
- Every section gets one clear job: prove, explain, qualify, or convert

Content order for the homepage:

1. Poster-style hero with demo artifact
2. Customer proof strip
3. Problem framing
4. Three explanatory value sections
5. ROI proof
6. Featured case study
7. Fit / qualification
8. Final CTA

## 6. Depth & Elevation

- Depth is subtle and layered
- Prefer soft, long shadows with low opacity
- Border plus shadow is the standard combination
- Use glow only when attached to signal colors or dark feature zones
- No generic floating-card soup

Recommended shadow character:

- Soft panel shadow for most cards
- Slightly stronger button shadow on hover
- Deep cinematic shadow only for major CTA and featured-case-study blocks

## 7. Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Keep the first screen calm, confident, and unmistakably about video intelligence
- Use mint selectively so it stays valuable
- Make the product demo the hero proof object
- Let whitespace and typography do most of the heavy lifting
- Use real semantic sections and strong focus states
- Keep copy direct, specific, and revenue-aware

### Do Not

- Do not drift into generic B2B SaaS card grids
- Do not use purple as the main accent
- Do not use Inter, system fonts, or default-looking stacks
- Do not overload the hero with stats, logos, badges, and multiple CTAs
- Do not make dark sections feel neon or gamer-ish
- Do not add visual noise that is not helping hierarchy or mood

## 8. Responsive Behavior

- Mobile should preserve the poster-like feeling, not collapse into cramped utility UI
- Fixed bars remain, but content should breathe beneath them
- Grids collapse cleanly to one column with strong vertical rhythm
- Buttons become full-width only when it genuinely improves usability
- Maintain generous corner radii and surface polish on mobile
- Product demo should still be the anchor on smaller screens

## 9. Agent Prompt Guide

Use these principles when extending the site:

- "Warm editorial tech aesthetic with mint signal color and dark forest CTA zones."
- "Poster-style hero with one dominant product artifact, not a dashboard collage."
- "Near-black typography on a mineral cream canvas with mono metadata accents."
- "Use soft borders, laminated surfaces, and restrained depth."
- "Write copy like an operator who understands churn, support load, and content discoverability."

Quick guardrails:

- If a section feels like interchangeable SaaS furniture, simplify it.
- If a color is not mint, cobalt, brass, cream, ink, or forest, it probably does not belong.
- If the design works without shadows, it is probably right.
